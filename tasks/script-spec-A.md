## SECTION OUTLINES (5 sections, ~320 words each)

### Section 1 — "The Half-Second Before You Choose"
- The Libet experiment (Benjamin Libet, 1983, *Brain*) — readiness potential precedes conscious intention by ~350-500 ms.
- The original Bereitschaftspotential (Kornhuber & Deecke, 1965).
- The Libet "veto" defense, why it never quite landed.
- Close on: the felt experience of choosing is downstream of something already in motion.

### Section 2 — "The Predictive Brain"
- Soon et al. (2008, *Nature Neuroscience*) — fMRI can predict simple choices up to 7-10 seconds before subject reports awareness.
- Schurger, Sitt & Dehaene (2012, *PNAS*) — the readiness potential may reflect stochastic accumulator noise, not a "decision command". Reframe: not predetermined, but pre-felt.
- Friston (2010, *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*) — the free-energy / predictive coding view: the brain is a prediction machine. Your sense of agency is itself a prediction.
- Close on: "decision" isn't a moment, it's a forecast that becomes a confession.

### Section 3 — "The Industries That Sell You Willpower"
- Self-help and productivity culture — "just choose differently". Cite Mischel marshmallow tradition (Mischel, Shoda & Rodriguez, 1989, *Science*) and its 2018 replication failure (Watts, Duncan & Quan, 2018, *Psychological Science*) showing socioeconomic confounds dominate.
- Wegner (2002) *The Illusion of Conscious Will* — the experience of willing is constructed after the fact.
- Sapolsky (2023) *Determined* — the case against contracausal free will, distributed across genes, hormones, environment.
- Close on: the will-power industry depends on hiding what neuroscience already knows.

### Section 4 — "The Decisions This Is Quietly Hijacking"
- Addiction and impulse: Volkow et al. work on dopamine deficits (Volkow, Wang & Baler, 2011, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*) — the felt "I chose to use" arrives after the brain has already committed.
- Advertising and choice architecture: Thaler & Sunstein (2008) *Nudge* — your "decision" is the output of a default someone else picked.
- Voting and snap moral judgments: Haidt (2001, *Psychological Review*) the social intuitionist model — moral reasoning is mostly post-hoc.
- Close on: the dignity of "I decided" is doing more work than the data supports.

### Section 5 — "How To Get Behind The Curtain"
- Practical compatibilist response — Mele (2014) *Free*. The neural priority of unconscious processes doesn't dissolve responsibility; it relocates the leverage.
- Pre-commitment & friction design (Ulysses contracts) — Elster (1979) *Ulysses and the Sirens*.
- Metacognition / interoception training — Farb et al. (2007, *Social Cognitive and Affective Neuroscience*) — mindfulness changes the gap between impulse and report.
- Identity-level over choice-level — "I am the kind of person who…" beats "I will choose to…". Reference behavioral activation logic.
- Close on: you don't outrun the half-second, you design the system that fills it.

## OUTRO BEATS
- The lab in 1983 didn't kill free will; it killed a fairy tale about it.
- What it gave you instead is more useful: knowing where the leverage actually lives.
- Sign-off line.
