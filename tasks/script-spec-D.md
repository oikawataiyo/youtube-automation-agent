## SECTION OUTLINES (5 sections, ~320 words each)

### Section 1 — "The Syndrome With A Name"
- Dorothy Tennov coined the term *limerence* in **Tennov (1979) — Love and Limerence (Stein and Day)**. Three core features: intrusive thinking, intense longing for reciprocation, mood that swings entirely on the target's signals.
- Distinguish from love. Love tolerates absence. Limerence cannot.
- Wakin & Vo (2008) — Reconsidering Limerence (*Journal of Marriage and Family Review* — note: the paper appeared in a marriage-and-family-themed review collection; cite as "Wakin and Vo, 2008, in a 2008 review of limerence research") — characterizes the cognitive signature: ~85% of waking thought aimed at the target during peak phase.
- Close on: there is a clinical word for what you are doing at 2 a.m., and naming it is the first thing that loosens its grip.

### Section 2 — "The Attachment Engine Underneath"
- Bowlby's attachment system explains why proximity-seeking spikes under uncertainty. Cite **Bowlby (1969) — Attachment (Basic Books)** and Ainsworth's Strange Situation framework (**Ainsworth, Blehar, Waters & Wall, 1978 — Patterns of Attachment, Lawrence Erlbaum**).
- Adult attachment translation: **Hazan & Shaver (1987) — Romantic love conceptualized as an attachment process (*Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*)** — anxious attachment styles report higher idealization, lower threshold for "falling".
- **Fraley & Shaver (2000) — Adult romantic attachment: theoretical developments, emerging controversies, and unanswered questions (*Review of General Psychology*)** — anxiously attached adults show hyperactivated attachment behavior under ambiguity, exactly the signal modern dating manufactures.
- Close on: limerence is the attachment system firing at a person who hasn't yet earned that level of activation from your nervous system.

### Section 3 — "Intermittent Reinforcement Is The Fuel"
- Skinner's variable-ratio schedule produces the most persistent behavior — **Ferster & Skinner (1957) — Schedules of Reinforcement (Appleton-Century-Crofts)**.
- Modern dating channels deliver replies on exactly this schedule: unpredictable timing, unpredictable warmth. Cite **Fisher, Aron & Brown (2005) — Romantic love: an fMRI study of a neural mechanism for mate choice (*Journal of Comparative Neurology*)** — the early intense-attraction phase shows VTA / caudate activity overlapping with reward-prediction circuits, not with the calm-attachment circuits of long-term pair bonds.
- Variable rewards do not feel like manipulation from the inside. They feel like meaning.
- Close on: the texture of "is it real this time" is the texture of a Skinner box your phone happens to be running.

### Section 4 — "What This Is Quietly Doing To Your Life"
- Cognitive load: intrusive thought eats working memory. Reference **Klinger (1977) — Meaning and Void (University of Minnesota Press)** on current-concern theory — unfulfilled goals capture attention.
- Sleep disruption and rumination spillover into depressive symptoms. Cite **Nolen-Hoeksema (2000) — The role of rumination in depressive disorders and mixed anxiety/depressive symptoms (*Journal of Abnormal Psychology*)**.
- Decision distortion at the relationship level: limerent attention narrows the field, suppresses information about incompatibility. Reference **Aron, Aron & Smollan (1992) — Inclusion of Other in the Self Scale (*Journal of Personality and Social Psychology*)** for how rapid self-other merging short-circuits assessment.
- Close on: the cost of limerence is not the heartbreak at the end. It is the months of attention you spent not being available for your own life.

### Section 5 — "How To Actually Get Out"
- Mikulincer & Shaver work on attachment security priming — security-prime imagery and routines down-regulate the activation loop. Reference **Mikulincer & Shaver (2007) — Attachment in Adulthood: Structure, Dynamics, and Change (Guilford Press)**.
- Behavioral structure that breaks the variable schedule: scheduled, predictable contact, or no contact. The mechanism mirrors exposure-and-response prevention for OCD-style intrusive thinking — **Foa & Kozak (1986) — Emotional processing of fear (*Psychological Bulletin*)**.
- Identity-level reframe — you are not "in love", you are mid-activation. Distance dissolves limerence within ~18-36 months even without intervention (Tennov field data).
- Close on: you do not have to win them. You have to extinguish the schedule.
