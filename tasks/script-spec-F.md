## SECTION OUTLINES (5 sections, ~320 words each)

### Section 1 — "The Cyberball Result"
- **Eisenberger, Lieberman & Williams (2003) — Does rejection hurt? An fMRI study of social exclusion (*Science*)** — the Cyberball paradigm. Excluded subjects show elevated activity in the dorsal anterior cingulate cortex (dACC) and anterior insula — the same regions recruited by physical pain.
- Replication and extension — **Eisenberger (2012) — The pain of social disconnection (*Nature Reviews Neuroscience*)**.
- The interpretation is not "it feels like pain". It is "the pain alarm system is firing".
- Close on: the brain does not have a separate file cabinet for hurt feelings.

### Section 2 — "Why The Overlap Exists"
- Evolutionary frame: for a social mammal, exclusion from the group was historically lethal. The pain system was the cheapest available alarm. Reference **Panksepp (1998) — *Affective Neuroscience* (Oxford University Press)** for the cross-species lineage of the social-pain system.
- **MacDonald & Leary (2005) — Why does social exclusion hurt? The relationship between social and physical pain (*Psychological Bulletin*)** — synthesis of the shared-systems hypothesis.
- Tylenol study — **DeWall, MacDonald, Webster, Masten, Baumeister, Powell, Combs, Schurtz, Stillman, Tice & Eisenberger (2010) — Acetaminophen reduces social pain (*Psychological Science*)** — three weeks of daily acetaminophen blunted both physical pain *and* hurt-feelings reports. The mechanism is shared infrastructure, not metaphor.
- Close on: rejection and a stubbed toe are running through partially overlapping wiring, and the wiring does not know the difference.

### Section 3 — "Loneliness As A Chronic Threat State"
- **Cacioppo & Hawkley (2009) — Perceived social isolation and cognition (*Trends in Cognitive Sciences*)** — chronic loneliness shifts the brain toward threat surveillance. Faces are scanned for rejection cues, neutral expressions read as cold.
- **Hawkley & Cacioppo (2010) — Loneliness matters: a theoretical and empirical review (*Annals of Behavioral Medicine*)** — physiological consequences: elevated cortisol, disrupted sleep architecture, increased systemic inflammation.
- **Holt-Lunstad, Smith & Layton (2010) — Social relationships and mortality risk: a meta-analytic review (*PLOS Medicine*)** — chronic loneliness is associated with roughly the all-cause mortality impact of smoking 15 cigarettes a day.
- Close on: this is the part where the conversation has to stop being polite.

### Section 4 — "Why Modern Architecture Makes It Worse"
- Park, Twenge & Greenfield research line on adolescent social media use and loneliness — cite **Twenge, Spitzberg & Campbell (2019) — Less in-person social interaction with peers among U.S. adolescents in the 21st century and links to loneliness (*Journal of Social and Personal Relationships*)**.
- Parasocial substitution — feels like connection at the consumption layer, returns no reciprocation signal to the social-pain system. Reference **Cacioppo, Hawkley & Berntson (2003) — The anatomy of loneliness (*Current Directions in Psychological Science*)**.
- Remote work and "weak tie" loss — cite **Yang, Holtz, Jaffe, Suri, Sinha, Weston, Joyce, Shah, Sherman, Hecht & Teevan (2022) — The effects of remote work on collaboration among information workers (*Nature Human Behaviour*)** — fewer cross-network bridges, narrower communication.
- Close on: the social-pain system evolved for tribes of 50 and is now being asked to read DM read-receipts. It does not handle it well.

### Section 5 — "What Actually Quiets The Alarm"
- High-quality contact beats high-quantity. Reference **Hall (2019) — How many hours does it take to make a friend? (*Journal of Social and Personal Relationships*)** — roughly 50 hours of shared time for friendship, ~200 for close friendship.
- Weak ties as low-cost social-pain dampeners — **Sandstrom & Dunn (2014) — Social interactions and well-being: the surprising power of weak ties (*Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin*)** — brief positive exchanges with baristas and dog walkers measurably lift subjective well-being.
- Behavioral activation logic: act first, the social-pain system updates from data, not from intention. Reference **Martell, Dimidjian & Herman-Dunn (2010) — *Behavioral Activation for Depression* (Guilford Press)** for the operating principle.
- Identity reframe — loneliness is hunger, not failure. The signal is asking for an input, not indicting your worth.
- Close on: you cannot think your way out of a pain signal. You can answer it.
