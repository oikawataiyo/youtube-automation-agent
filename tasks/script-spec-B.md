## SECTION OUTLINES (5 sections, ~320 words each)

### Section 1 — "The Map We Got Wrong"
- Origin of the serotonin/monoamine hypothesis (Schildkraut, 1965, *American Journal of Psychiatry*) — drug action retro-fitted to a theory.
- Moncrieff et al. (2022, *Molecular Psychiatry*) — the systematic umbrella review showing no consistent evidence for a serotonin deficiency in depression.
- Why the theory lived so long anyway — the marketing of SSRIs, the lay-public ease of "low serotonin".
- Close on: the chemical-imbalance story was scaffolding, not biology.

### Section 2 — "The Predictive Brain Theory"
- Friston (2010, *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*) — the free-energy principle: the brain minimizes prediction error.
- Barrett & Simmons (2015, *Nature Reviews Neuroscience*) — interoceptive predictive coding; mood is a forecast about the body's expected energy budget.
- Stephan et al. (2016, *Lancet Psychiatry*) — depression as allostatic dysregulation; the brain's prediction of future reward / energy collapses.
- Close on: depression isn't "low mood" — it's the brain budgeting for a future it has stopped believing in.

### Section 3 — "The Body That Won't Stop Burning"
- Raison & Miller (2011, *Current Psychiatry Reports*) — inflammation-depression link.
- Felger (2019, *Pharmacology & Therapeutics*) — peripheral inflammation alters basal ganglia dopamine and motivation circuits.
- Köhler-Forsberg et al. (2019, *Acta Psychiatrica Scandinavica*) — anti-inflammatory adjuncts in depression meta-analysis.
- Close on: the "mood" you can't shake is sometimes an immune system in the wrong key.

### Section 4 — "Why 'Just Be Positive' Backfires"
- Treatment-resistant depression rates — STAR*D trial (Rush et al., 2006, *American Journal of Psychiatry*) ~33% remission after first antidepressant.
- Wegner (1994, *Psychological Review*) — ironic process theory: trying not to feel a thing makes the thing more available.
- Wood et al. (2009, *Psychological Science*) — repeating positive affirmations can lower self-esteem in people who don't believe them.
- Close on: forcing a prediction the brain doesn't buy widens the error, not closes it.

### Section 5 — "What Actually Reaches The Circuit"
- Behavioral activation (Lewinsohn, 1974; Dimidjian et al., 2006, *Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology*) — schedule action, let mood follow. Why it works: it forces small predictions to be confirmed.
- Ketamine and rapid-acting antidepressants (Berman et al., 2000, *Biological Psychiatry*; Krystal et al., 2019, *Cell*) — NMDA-glutamate route, fast plasticity. Reframes the system from monoamine tinkering to predictive recalibration.
- Sleep, light, movement — Walker (2017) on sleep deprivation as a depression amplifier; the basics that fix the prediction substrate.
- Therapy choice — cognitive behavioral therapy works when it updates predictions, not just thoughts (Beck, 1979 lineage).
- Close on: you don't argue with depression. You feed the model evidence it can no longer ignore.

## OUTRO BEATS
- The serotonin story let us treat depression as a missing nutrient. The predictive-brain story tells us it's an updated forecast.
- That's harder. It's also more hopeful — forecasts can be retrained.
- Sign-off.
